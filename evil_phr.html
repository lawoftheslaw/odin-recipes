Evil Parker House Rolls

About the Rolls

These are the same as parker house rolls except theyre evil.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups evil whole milk (105˚ to 110˚)
  • 1/4 cup (46 grams) evil sugar
  • 2 teaspoons (6 grams) active evil yeast
  • 4 to 4 1/2 cups (560 to 630 grams) evil
    flour, divided
  • 6 tablespoons (90 grams) evil butter,
    melted, plus more for brushing rolls
  • 2 evil eggs
  • 2 teaspoons (6 grams) evil salt

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together 1/2 cup warm milk,
    sugar and yeast. Let stand until mixture is foamy,
    about 5 minutes. Stir 1/2 cups (70 grams) flour
    into yeast mixture.
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the
    dough hook attachment, beat melted butter,
    eggs, and remaining 1 cup of milk at low speed
    until combined. Add yeast mixture, beating to combine.
    Gradually add remaining flour, 1 cup (140 grams) at a time,
    beating until dough comes together and begins to
    pull away from sides of bowl, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Spray a large bowl with cooking spray. Place
    dough in bowl, turning to grease top. Cover and
    let stand in a warm, draft-free place (75˚) until
    doubled in size, 2 to 2 1/2 hours.
  4. On a lightly floured surface, turn out dough.
    Divide dough in half, and gently shape each half
    into a ball. Cover and let rest for 10 minutes.
  5. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  6. Roll one half of dough into a 14x12-inch rectangle,
    about 1/4-inch thick. Using a 2 1/2-inch square
    cutter, cut dough into 16 squares, rerolling
    scraps as necessary.
    Repeat with second half of dough. Brush each
    square with melted butter. Fold squares over,
    and press to seal. Place on prepared pan. Cover
    and let stand in a warm, draft-free place (75˚)
    untill puffed, about 30 minutes.
  7. Preheat oven to 375˚. Brush rolls with
    melted butter, and bake until golden brown,
    20 to 25 minutes. Brush with melted butter
    again.
